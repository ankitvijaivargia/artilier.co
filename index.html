03 — Beyond the Three

When the brief calls for more.

The three pillars are what we make. Floor, Surface, Soft — that is the discipline of the studio. But the rooms designers ask us to dress are rarely served by textiles alone. They call for hard materials, fine finishing, considered furniture — work that lives at the edges of what we make ourselves.

For these, Artilier coordinates with master ateliers across India whose craft traditions are as rigorous as our own. One brief. One studio relationship. One delivery. The designer never has to source twice.

I
Hard materials
Stone, marble, terrazzo and inlay — for floors, surfaces and decorative panels — drawn from India's stone-craft traditions.
II
Fine metalwork
Brass, bronze, hand-beaten and hand-finished — hardware, fixtures, decorative metal — through metalsmiths whose work meets museum standard.
III
Wood & joinery
Hand-carved and inlaid wood, furniture, panelling — through master joiners working in the manner the architecture deserves.
IV
Leather & finishing
Hand-stitched leather upholstery, wrapped surfaces, decorative finishes — supplied to the studio's specification, finished to the studio's standard.

Beyond the three is not a catalogue. It is a position — that the designer should never have to manage a chain of suppliers when one studio can hold the brief and answer for the whole.
